## Technique Execution Results

### Role Playing — Teacher & Parent Perspectives

**Interactive Focus:** What teachers and parents want from parent-teacher communication; transparency and notification features.

**Key Breakthroughs:**

- **[PT-1] Score Transparency Portal**  
  _Concept:_ Parents can view child's exam/homework scores *and* see *why* they got those scores—explanations, rubrics, or AI-generated breakdowns. Moves beyond "you got a B" to actionable understanding.  
  _Novelty:_ Transparency on the *reasoning* behind scores, not just the number; reduces "why did my child get this grade?" anxiety.

- **[PT-2] Proactive Notification Subscription**  
  _Concept:_ Parents can set up notifications (e.g., when a test grade drops, homework submitted, or milestone reached). Teacher doesn't have to manually reach out.  
  _Novelty:_ Parent-driven subscription model—parents choose what they want to know, reducing reactive "when will we hear?" frustration.

- **[PT-3] Bidirectional Easy Messaging**  
  _Concept:_ Simple, built-in channel for parent-to-teacher and teacher-to-parent messaging. No need to chase email or school portals.  
  _Novelty:_ In-platform, context-aware (tied to student, assignment, or class) so conversations stay organized.

**Value Unlock:** Teacher saves time and gets fewer calls; parent avoids frustration with progress reports and communication—both sides win.

### Assumption Reversal — Beyond "Scores & Notifications"

**Assumption flipped:** "Parents mainly want scores and notifications" → **What do they actually want?**

**Key Breakthroughs:**

- **[AR-1] Comparative Benchmarking**  
  _Concept:_ Parent views child's metrics relative to class, district, state, and national benchmarks. Answers "How does my child stack up?" not just "What grade did they get?"  
  _Novelty:_ Layered comparison (class → state → country) in one view; contextualizes performance.

- **[AR-2] Longitudinal Archive & Improvement Trail**  
  _Concept:_ Store all exam/HW images and data as a permanent record. Show progression over time—charts, before/after, trend lines. Answers "Is my child improving?"  
  _Novelty:_ Persistent artifact archive + improvement narrative; not just current snapshot.

- **[AR-3] Consistency & Study Habits Tracker**  
  _Concept:_ Track behavioral patterns: missed class, missed HW, exam participation, project completion. Surface consistency metrics alongside academic scores.  
  _Novelty:_ Combines academic + behavioral data; predicts risk before grades drop.

- **[AR-4] Strengths & Weaknesses Diagnostic**  
  _Concept:_ Clear view of subject/topic strengths and gaps—not just grades, but *where* the child shines or struggles.  
  _Novelty:_ Actionable diagnostic; guides what to work on next.

- **[AR-5] Flexible Consumption (Push vs Pull)**  
  _Concept:_ Notifications configurable as daily, weekly, or monthly digest—or parent can skip notifications and just log in to dashboard when they want.  
  _Novelty:_ Respects parent preference (high-touch vs low-touch); reduces notification fatigue.

- **[AR-6] Self-Serve Parent & Student Access**  
  _Concept:_ Parent and student can access dashboard, benchmarks, and improvement data without teacher mediation. Portable record if student switches schools; parent-teacher convos focus on action, not "what happened."  
  _Novelty:_ Data ownership and access decoupled from teacher availability; student sees own progress.

### What If Scenarios — Boundary Expansion

- **[WI-1] Real-Time Parent View** — Live view of in-class work as it's submitted
- **[WI-2] Student-Owned Shareable Portfolio** — Student can share link with colleges, tutors, or new schools
- **[WI-3] AI Feedback on Every Assignment** — Unlimited AI: instant feedback, explanations, practice suggestions per assignment
- **[WI-4] Async-First Parent-Teacher Comms** — Voice memos, short videos instead of scheduling live meetings

### Role Playing — Student Perspective

**Student wants:**
- **Helping me win** — Feels like the platform is on their side, not surveilling
- **Showing my growth** — Visible progress they can be proud of (connects to longitudinal archive)
- **Giving me secret tips** — Unlockable hints, AI nudges, teacher insights they can use
- **Helping me level up** — Progression, mastery, RPG-like framing; student owns the journey

**Novelty:** Student-centric framing (agency, pride, empowerment) vs parent/teacher surveillance view.

### Role Playing — Principal Perspective

**Principal wants:**
- **Works for all kinds of students** — Inclusive design; not one-size-fits-all
- **Special needs support** — Accommodations, accessibility, differentiated paths, IEP alignment
- **Gifted/talented (smart kids) too** — Challenge, enrichment, not left bored or underserved
- **Cohort-level metrics for resource decisions** — See where most students are weak (e.g., math school-wide) so they can hire more resources for that department, adjust curriculum emphasis, or target professional development

**Novelty:** Full-spectrum equity + strategic data — principal uses aggregate insights for staffing, hiring, and resource allocation, not just individual student view.

### Role Playing — Org Manager / Tutoring Center

**Same as Principal dashboard, different org type:**
- **Overview of everything** — Principal, org manager, or tutoring center owner needs to see what's going on across all classrooms/teachers/students
- One org-level dashboard serves schools, districts, tutoring centers — oversight, cohort metrics, resource decisions

### Risks & Edge Cases

- **Privacy & data exposure** — Parents seeing other students; sensitive info in messages
- **Notification overload** — Too many alerts → parents tune out
- **Surveillance anxiety** — Students feel watched; parents obsess over every score
- **Data misuse** — Schools using cohort data punitively; vendor data handling
- **Inequality** — Parents without devices/time to log in; tech gap between families

**Mitigations:** Role-based access, configurable notification cadence, student visibility controls, data-use policies, offline/low-bandwidth options

- **Banking apps** — Spending breakdowns, alerts, "what changed" summaries (→ notification design, digest format)
- **Social parenting apps** — Community forums, milestone sharing, parent-to-parent support (→ internal community for teachers/parents)
